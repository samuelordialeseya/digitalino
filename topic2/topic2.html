Topic 2: Cyber Threats and Risks

By Pauline Bernadette Diaz

What is a Cyber Threat?

A cybersecurity threat is a malicious act that intends to steal information, damage data, and disrupt digital life. It may come from within an organization of trusted users, or from unauthorized parties. Cyber threats and risks are evolving as technology advances, and consequently cause significant challenges to individuals, businesses, and governments.

Common examples of threats and risks:

  • MALWARErefers to malicious software, such as viruses, worms, and ransom ware, which can infect devices and systems, causing data theft, disruption, and financial loss.
  • PHISHING refers to cybercriminals' use of deceptive emails, messages, or websites to trick users into revealing sensitive information like passwords and credit card details.
  • DISTRIBUTED DENIAL OF SERVICE (DDoS) Attacks refers to attackers that flood a network with traffic, causing it to become inaccessible, disrupting services and causing financial harm.
  • RANSOM WARE refers to when cybercriminals encrypt data and demand a ransom for its release, threatening data loss or public exposure if the ransom isn't paid.
  • INADEQUATE SECURITY PRACTICES refers to failure to update software, use strong passwords, or implement security measures that leave systems vulnerable to threats.

Conclusion

Cyber threats are everywhere, and anyone can be a target. Therefore, staying informed about emerging threats and employing incident response plans minimize the impact of cyber attacks. Collaboration between governments, and cyber security experts is crucial in the ongoing battle against cyber threats, as the landscape continues to evolve and adapt.
