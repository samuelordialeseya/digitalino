What To Do When You Encounter Cyber Threats

By Samuel Ordiales

Understanding and Addressing Cyber Threats

Encountering cyber threats can be stressful, and knowing how to respond to them makes a difference. To know if an account has been hacked or phished, there are specific warning sights such as Unusual System or Network Activity, Unauthorized Access Attempts, Unexpected Behavior, and Phishing Emails to indicate this. However, by practicing specific actions when dealing with cyber threats, individuals may stay safe, and calm.

Essential Phases to follow to respond, recover, and strengthen security:

  1. Act Fast, Do not Panic
  2. Notify Relevant People
  3. Recover: contain, clean, and rebuild
  4. Test and restore operations safely
  5. Post-attack review and resilience building

Simple, practical actions individuals can take to respond to cyber risks:

  • IF YOU RECEIVE A SUSPICIOUS MESSAGE: Don’t click any links. Don’t reply. Report or block the sender.
  • IF YOU THINK YOUR ACCOUNT WAS HACKED: Change your password immediately. Check for unfamiliar logins. Report to the platform
  • IF YOUR DEVICE IS INFECTED BY MALWARE: Disconnect from the internet. Run antivirus or malware removal tools. Seek professional help
  • IF YOU ENCOUNTER CYBERBULLYING: Don't respond. Screenshot. Report to the platform or trusted adults.
  • IF YOU FALL FOR A SCAM: Contact back instution. Change affected passwords. File a report.

Conclusion

Experiencing cyber threats can cause feelings of anxiousness, however, knowing how to respond to them enables individuals to take control of the situation. By quickly securing compromised accounts, reporting incidents, and seeking help from professionals, individuals take essential steps in containing the threat and protecting others. Through these actions, users contribute to a collective effort of maintaining a safer digital community.
