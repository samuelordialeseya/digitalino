Phishing: Don’t Take the Bait!

By Samuel Ordiales

Phishing

Phishing is a cyber attack that uses disguised email as a weapon. The goal is to trick the email recipient into believing that the message is something they want or need — a request from their bank, for instance, or a note from someone in their company — and to click a link or download an attachment.

How Phishing Works

Attackers often send emails that appear to come from trusted sources. These emails may contain links to fake websites or attachments that install malware.

How to Protect Yourself

  • Always check the sender's email address.
  • Do not click suspicious links or download unknown attachments.
  • Use two-factor authentication when possible.
  • Keep your software up to date.

Conclusion

Being aware of phishing tactics is the first step to protecting yourself online. Stay vigilant and educate others about these common threats.
